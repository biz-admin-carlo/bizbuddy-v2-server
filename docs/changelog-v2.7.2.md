## Bug Fixes

### 1. Auto Clock-Out Safeguard — Late Clock-Ins Incorrectly Triggered

> **Affects:** Server only (background cron)
> **Breaking:** No

**The bug:** The 5-hour trigger condition only checked whether `now >= shiftEnd + 5 hours`. It did not verify that the employee had actually been clocked in for 5 hours. Employees who clocked in late — within the last hour or two of their shift — would be auto-clocked out with only minutes or a fraction of an hour recorded, and incorrectly notified that they hit a "5-hour limit."

**The fix:** The cron now requires both conditions to be true before triggering:

1. `now >= resolvedTimeOut + 5 hours` — the session is genuinely overdue past shift end
2. `now >= timeIn + 5 hours` — the employee has actually been clocked in for 5 hours

Sessions that pass condition (1) but fail condition (2) — i.e. late clock-ins — are skipped entirely and left open for the next cron pass.

| | Before | After |
|---|---|---|
| Trigger condition | `now >= shiftEnd + 5h` only | `now >= shiftEnd + 5h` AND `now >= timeIn + 5h` |
| Late clock-in behavior | Clocked out with < 1 hour recorded | Skipped, session remains open |
| Email notification | Incorrectly stated "5-hour limit reached" | Not sent (condition not met) |

**File changed:** `src/jobs/autoClockOutSafeguard.js`

## Data Correction

### Affected Records

A total of **54 time log records** across 2 companies were incorrectly auto-clocked out by the pre-fix cron between **2026-04-02 and 2026-04-09**. All affected records had less than 5 hours of actual work recorded at the time of the erroneous clock-out.

| Company | Logs Affected | Avg. Hours Recorded |
|---|---|---|
| Company A | 45 | 0.91 hrs |
| Company B | 9 | 1.93 hrs |

### Remediation Steps Taken

1. **SQL revert** — All 54 logs were reset: `timeOut` cleared, `status` reopened (`true`), `autoClockOut` and `autoClockOutAt` cleared. Any coffee or lunch breaks force-closed by the cron were also reopened.
2. **Verification** — Post-revert query confirmed `COUNT(*) = 0` for logs matching the broken condition.
3. **Supervisor notification** — Affected sessions are now open and flagged for supervisor review to set the correct clock-out time.
4. **Employee notification** — A one-time correction email was sent to all affected employees via `src/jobs/sendAutoClockOutCorrectionEmails.js` informing them of the correction and directing them to their time logs.

## 🐛 Bug Fixes

### 1. Auto Clock-Out Safeguard — Wrong 5-Hour Trigger Baseline

> **Affects:** Server only (background cron)
> **Breaking:** No

**The bug:** The 5-hour safeguard was measuring elapsed time from `timeIn`, not from the employee's scheduled shift end. This caused all employees to be auto clocked out after only 5 hours of work — well before their shift was over.

**The fix:** The cron now resolves each session's `resolvedTimeOut` (scheduled shift end, or `timeIn + defaultShiftHours` as fallback) first, then only triggers if `now > resolvedTimeOut + 5 hours`. Sessions that have not yet exceeded 5 hours past their supposed clock-out time are skipped entirely.

| | Before | After |
|---|---|---|
| Trigger condition | `timeIn` is 5+ hours ago | `now` is 5+ hours past `resolvedTimeOut` |
| Side effect | Employees auto clocked out mid-shift | Only triggers for genuinely abandoned sessions |

No changes to the `timeOut` value written, the `autoClockOut` flag, supervisor review requirement, or notification shape.
